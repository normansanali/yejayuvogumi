Randy Fullmer cause of death, obituary, funeral

September 2024 · 3 minute read

This report highlights Randy Fullmer’s cause of death, obituary and funeral following his sad death which occurred on July 10, 2023.

Randall Fullmer was an American businessman and executive for The Walt Disney Company. After a career at Walt Disney Feature Animation, Fullmer launched his own business, Wyn Guitars.

On April 27, 1950, Fullmer was born in Richland, Washington. His mother was a physical therapist, while his father was a nuclear physicist.

From 1968 to 1970, Fullmer attended Washington State University (WSU) to pursue a two-year architecture degree.

He attended a film class in his second year at WSU and became hopelessly addicted to animation; this inspired him to apply to the California Institute of the Arts (Cal Arts). Fullmer received acceptance into Cal Arts’ animation department and earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts in 1974.

Following his graduation from Cal Arts, Fullmer had his own animation company for almost seven years, creating animations for films about medicine, science, and other subjects, as well as for Sesame Street segments, television commercials, and Saturday morning cartoons.

Fullmer worked at Don Bluth Studios in 1983 and 1984, producing special effects for the first Laserdisc video games, Space Ace and Dragon’s Lair. From 1985 to 1987, Fullmer animated for both television and motion pictures at Filmation. He had previously worked at Apogee, John Dykstra’s live-action special effects studio. He contributed to films including Ghostbusters, BraveStarr, She-Ra: Princess of Power, and Happily Ever After.

When Fullmer was six years old, he first began trombone instruction. When Fullmer was twelve years old, he begged his parents for permission to purchase a 12-string guitar to go along with his 6-string electric guitar. Fullmer requested if he could buy the wood to make his own 12-string guitar after they declined due to the fact that he already owned a guitar.

Approximately 30 guitars were built over the course of the following six years by Fullmer, who learned the art from both self-teaching and Tom, an old country western fiddle maker. (Fullmer kept on building guitars for the rest of his life, but it was put on the back burner while his animation profession took priority.)

Randy Fullmer’s cause of death, obituary, funeral

Randy Fullmer’s cause of death has been attributed to cancer which he battled for a very long time. He died in Los on July 10, 2023, at the age of 73. Funeral arrangements are meanwhile pending. Additionally, an obituary confirming his death has been shared on social media and verifiable websites.

Randy Fullmer founded Wyn Guitars and served as its lone luthier. He created hundreds of distinctive basses for musicians like Jimmy Haslip of the Yellowjackets and Jermaine Jackson. Following a well-received display of Wyn Guitar enthusiasts at the National Association of Music Merchants convention in 2011, Fullmer was the subject of a documentary by Mike Enns about Wyn’s influence on the music business, which resulted in a nearly 200-person waitlist for custom bass guitars.
